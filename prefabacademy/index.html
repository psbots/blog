Week 1 : Introduction and Setup

Pre-FabAcademy is a 1 month course prior to the annual FabAcademy, which aims to give a headstart to the newly instated FabLabs in Kochi and Trivandrum in the state of Kerala. The programme includes hands on training on all the machines as well as introduction to version control, electronics, programming and design softwares. This website serves to document my experience participating in the Pre-FabAcademy Kochi 2015.

Week 1 involved getting to know fellow participants and the instructor. We were guided through the various software setup required for successfully completing FabAcademy. The weeks activities included installing and getting familiar with :

  • version control software - mercurial and git
  • video conferencing softwares - LinPhone and Cisco Jabber Video
  • virtualbox ubuntu setup
  • 2D and 3D design softwares

Day 1

Day 1 started off with all the participants introducing themselves. We were fortunate to have someone like Luciano Betoldi who has a lot of experience in running the FabLab at Barcelona as our instructor.

Luciano told us about the FabAcademy program, how it was very challenging but rewarding as well. He mentioned the various topics that would be covered by Neil during the 6 month long classes and gave us a hint about the weekly assignments.

He also showed a few examples of Final projects made by participants of earlier FabAcademy runs and showed us the websites for documentation.

Then he moved on to explain about the Pre-FabAcademy, its need and aim.

The equipments and consumables in the lab were in bit of a dis-order, and we spent the rest of the day rearranging and reorganising the lab for improving our productivity in the coming days as recommended by Luciano.

Day 2

Day 2 started off with Luciano telling us about the software setup used in FabAcademy. He asked all of us to get Ubuntu running on our laptops. Since I'm using a Mac, I found it better to get Ubuntu running on a VirtualBox instead of installing it in the hard-disk.

It has been a while since I've used Ubuntu, so the installation and setup acted like a refresher for me. Tried out the basic commands on the terminal.

  • sudo apt-get update : To update all the ubuntu packages
  • sudo apt-get upgrade : To upgrade all the software to the latest version
  • cd : To change directory
  • ls : To list the contents of the current directory
  • mkdir : To make a directory
  • pwd : To print working directory

Day 5

On Day 5, Luciano came along with our second instructor Francisco, who would be engaging us in the last week of the Pre-FabAcademy. Luciano continued with the previous day's topic of CAD Design with Rhino. An assignment was given the previous day and the submission was discussed. I tried out the assignment in the class, trying to catch up yesterday's work.

Francisco introduced yet another CAD software called Antimony. He explained the key difference between Antimony and other CAD software. Antimony is the only software that gives exact geometric representation of a 3D model instead of finite element approximations. The distinguishing features of Antimony are :

  • Geometric Function representation
  • Hierarchical Design
  • Parametric Design

I liked Antimony for its simplicity and focus on mathematical accuracy. The software though not suited for aethetic/organic design, is very powerful for creating mechanical component design. Francisco showed us a demo for a bit-holder in Antimony. I went ahead and made a cylindrical version of the bit-holder.

I caught up with yesterday's Rhino with some help from Sibu, one of the Pre-FabAcademy participants. I plan to look more into Rhino during the weekend.
